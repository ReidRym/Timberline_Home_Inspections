    Buyer Home Inspection

     During a home inspection, a buyer can expect a comprehensive examination of the property's structural integrity, electrical systems, plumbing, and overall condition. We will provide a detailed report outlining any issues, potential repairs, or safety concerns, aiding the buyer in making an informed decision about the purchase.

    Pre-Listing Inspection

     In a pre-listing inspection, a seller can anticipate a thorough assessment of the property's condition before listing it for sale. This preemptive inspection provides sellers with insights into potential issues, allowing them to address concerns proactively and enhance the marketability of their home.

    New Construction Inspection

     In a new construction inspection, the buyer can anticipate a detailed assessment of the recently built home to ensure it meets quality standards and adheres to local building codes. We will meticulously examine the construction work, plumbing, electrical systems, and other components to identify any defects or issues that may need attention, providing the buyer with peace of mind regarding the newly constructed property. These typically outline cosmetic defects as well.

    12 Month Warranty Inspection

     The homeowner can expect a thorough examination of the property to identify any issues that may be covered by the builder's warranty. We will assess the home's systems and components to ensure that any defects or concerns are addressed before the warranty expires, providing the owner with added assurance and an opportunity to address issues with the builder.

    Sewer Scope

     In a sewer scope inspection, a buyer can expect a specialized examination of the property's sewer line using a camera. This thorough inspection allows the home inspector to identify any potential issues, such as blockages or damage, providing the buyer with crucial information about the condition of the sewer system before making a purchase decision.

    Radon Inspection

     During a radon inspection, a buyer can expect an examination of the property to assess the levels of radon gas. We will employ specialized testing equipment to determine if radon concentrations are within acceptable limits, ensuring the buyer is informed about potential health risks associated with radon exposure in the home.

    Aerial Roof Inspection

     A buyer can anticipate a comprehensive assessment of the property's roof on all inspections. We do our best to walk every safely accessible roof, but sometimes it is not safe to do so, or the roof material cannot be walked. In these instances we perform a comprehensive evaluation of the roof using a drone, at no additional charge to the buyer. This innovative approach allows us to capture detailed images and footage, providing a thorough analysis of the roof's condition, potential damage, and overall structural integrity from a unique perspective.

    Pool & Spa

     The buyer can expect a detailed examination of the facilities to assess their overall condition, safety features, and compliance with local regulations. We inspect the pool and spa components, including pumps, filters, electrical systems, and barriers, providing valuable insights into any necessary repairs or maintenance required for a safe and enjoyable aquatic environment.

    Termite

     In a termite/wood destroying organism inspection, a buyer can expect a thorough examination of the property to identify any current or previous signs of termite activity or damage. We will inspect key areas, such as the foundation, attic, and interior areas, providing the buyer with critical information about the presence of termites and potential risks to the property's integrity.
