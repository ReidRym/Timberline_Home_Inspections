    • Timberline Services

    "Unlocking Peace of Mind in Every Corner of Your Home"

    Buyer Home Inspection

    During a home inspection, a buyer can expect a comprehensive examination
    of the property's structural integrity, electrical systems, plumbing, and overall condition. A skilled Timberline inspector will provide a detailed report outlining any issues, potential repairs, or safety concerns, aiding the buyer in making an informed decision about the purchase.

    Pre-Listing Inspection

    In a pre-listing inspection, a buyer can anticipate a thorough assessment of the property's condition before listing it for sale. This preemptive inspection
    provides sellers with insights into potential issues, allowing them to address concerns proactively and enhance the marketability of their home.

    New Construction Inspection

    In a new construction inspection, a buyer can anticipate a detailed assessment of the recently built home to ensure it meets quality standards and adheres to local building codes. Timberline inspector will meticulously examine the construction work, plumbing, electrical systems, and other components to identify any defects or issues that may need attention, providing the buyer with peace of mind regarding the newly constructed property.

    12 Month Warranty Inspection

    A buyer can expect a thorough examination of the property to identify any issues covered by the builder's warranty. The Timberline inspector will assess the home's systems and components to ensure that any defects or concerns are addressed before the warranty expires, providing the buyer
    with added assurance and an opportunity to address issues with the builder.

    Sewer Scope

    In a sewer scope inspection, a buyer can expect a specialized examination
    of the property's sewer line using a camera. This thorough inspection allows the home inspector to identify any potential issues, such as blockages or damage, providing the buyer with crucial information about the condition of the sewer system before making a purchase decision.

    Radon Inspection

    During a radon inspection, a buyer can expect a meticulous examination of the property to assess the levels of radon gas. The Timberline inspector will employ specialized testing equipment to determine if radon concentrations are within acceptable limits, ensuring the buyer is informed about potential health risks associated with radon exposure in the home.

    Aerial Roof Inspection

    A buyer can anticipate a comprehensive assessment of the property's roof using drone technology. This innovative approach allows the Timberline inspector to
    capture detailed images and footage, providing a thorough analysis of the roof's condition, potential damage, and overall structural integrity from a unique perspective.

    Pool & Spa

    A buyer can expect a detailed examination of the facilities to assess their overall condition, safety features, and compliance with local regulations. The
    Timberline inspector will inspect the pool and spa components, including pumps, filters, electrical systems, and barriers, providing valuable insights into any necessary repairs or maintenance required for a safe and enjoyable aquatic environment.

    Termite

    In a termite inspection, a buyer can expect a thorough examination of the property to identify any signs of termite activity or damage. The Timberline inspector will inspect key areas, such as the foundation, wood structures, and crawl spaces, providing the buyer with critical information about the presence of termites and potential risks to the property's integrity.
